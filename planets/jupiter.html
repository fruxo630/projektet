About Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet in the solar system and is also the largest planet in the entire system. It is a gas giant much like its counterpart Saturn, with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets combined, but still a little bit less than that of the Sun. It's the third brightest planet from Earth after the Moon and Venus and has been observed countless of times through history. It's named after the Roman god of jupiter who is also known as the king of the gods, because of its massive size.

There has been one spacecraft that has visited Jupiter, making a close approach to the planet in 1973 and has since been explored on many occasions by robotic spacecrafts. Jupiter also has a planetary ring system though it's very faint. Furthermore it also has almost a hundred known moons and probably even more than that number that just haven't been discovered in this current time.
