About Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet in our system. Its name is a reference to the Greek god of the sky, Uranus who in this case according to Greek mythology, was the great-grandfather of Ares, grandfather of Zeus and father of Cronus.

Because Uranus is classified as an ice giant, it's already pretty obvious that it is one of the coldest planets in the solar system. It's not the same "gas giant" as with Jupiter or Saturn because even though it also consists of hydrogen and helium like the others, it also contains more "ices" such as a lot more water. Much like with the other giant planets, Uranus also has a ring system like with Saturn and multiple moons.
