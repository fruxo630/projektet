About Saturn

Saturn is our sixth planet in the solar system and also the second largest in the solar system, after Jupiter. It is a gas giant with an average radius of almost nine and a half times that of Earth so it's a very large planet. Even though the planets density is less than Earth, because of its larger volume, it's almost 95 times more massive. Saturn is named after the Roman god of wealth and agriculture.

Even with all of this, Saturn is most famous for its ring system, which is mainly composed of ice particles, with smaller amounts of rocky debris and dust. Along with this, Saturn is also the one planet with the most amount of moons in the entire solar system. Having a total of 82 moons that are at least known to orbit around the planet, of which 53 of them are officially named. One of the more interesting facts is that Titan, which is Saturn's largest moon and the second largest one in the entire solar system, is actually larger than the planet Mercury, which is crazy to think about.
