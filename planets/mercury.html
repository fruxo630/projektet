About Mercury

Mercury is the first planet in the solar system, the closest one to the Sun and also the smallest planet in the entire system. It is named after the Roman god Mercurius, god of commerce, messenger of the gods and mediator between gods and mortals. A total of 2 spacecrafts have visited Mercury in our time where one of them eventually exhausted its fuel and crashed into the planet's surface.

One of the things that make Mercury very interesting is that it rotates in a way that is completely unique in the Solar System. It is tidally locked with the sun or otherwise known as the planet always having the same face toward the object that it is orbiting around, in this case the Sun. This means compared to the fixed stars, the ones that doesn't move in the night sky, Mercury rotates exactly three times for every two times that it makes around the Sun.
